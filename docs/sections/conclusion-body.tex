\section{Conclusion}

This project implemented a reduced-form credit simulation framework for pricing structured credit-linked notes under stochastic hazard-rate dynamics. The model combines deterministic input curves, HJM-style forward hazard evolution, pathwise survival weighting, and Monte Carlo estimators for vanilla and range-accrual CLN payoffs.

The implementation focuses on the numerical mechanics required for structured credit Monte Carlo. The range–accrual CLN payoff is represented through coupon, recovery, and redemption legs, with the redemption amount linked to pathwise model CDS spreads. This structure provides a useful test case for comparing baseline simulation against variance reduction methods while keeping the modelling assumptions transparent.

The numerical results support the internal consistency of the implementation. The HJM hazard engine reprices the input survival curves within Monte Carlo error, and the baseline estimators exhibit the expected convergence behaviour as the number of simulated paths increases. The investment grade and high yield cases also produce the expected ordering of present values, with the high yield note priced lower because higher hazard rates imply larger expected credit losses.

Variance reduction improves estimator stability in this setup. Antithetic variates deliver the strongest and most consistent reduction in standard errors, while the vanilla-CLN control variate provides a more modest improvement. The weaker control variate performance reflects the realised payoff structure under the chosen barrier level: the range-accrual feature does not generate enough independent variation relative to the vanilla CLN for the control to dominate antithetic sampling.

Natural extensions include importance sampling for rare-credit-event regimes, quasi–Monte Carlo methods for smoother pathwise estimators, and a JAX implementation for vectorised simulation, automatic differentiation, and GPU acceleration. A fuller market implementation would also replace the simplified model CDS proxy with par-spread calculations based on premium and protection legs, calibrated to traded CDS and discount curves.
